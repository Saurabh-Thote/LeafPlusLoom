How to Care for Your Wooden Spoon Set

With proper care, your bamboo cooking spoons will last for years! Follow these simple steps to maintain their beauty and functionality:

üíß Daily Cleaning

  1. Rinse immediately after use with warm (not hot) water
  2. Wash with mild dish soap and soft sponge
  3. Rinse thoroughly to remove all soap residue
  4. Dry immediately with clean towel
  5. Stand upright in holder to air dry completely

üõ¢Ô∏è Monthly Conditioning (Important!)

Why condition? Regular oiling prevents drying, cracking, and maintains the natural luster of bamboo.

  1. Ensure spoons are completely clean and dry
  2. Apply food-grade mineral oil or coconut oil with soft cloth
  3. Rub oil following the wood grain direction
  4. Let oil absorb for 4-6 hours (or overnight)
  5. Wipe off excess oil with clean cloth

üè† Storage Tips

  • Use the bamboo holder: Stores spoons upright with proper ventilation
  • Keep away from heat sources: Avoid storing near stoves or ovens
  • Avoid damp areas: Store in dry, well-ventilated kitchen area
  • Never stack wet spoons: Always dry completely before storing

‚ùå Never Do These:

  • ‚ùå Don't put in dishwasher - High heat and harsh detergents will damage bamboo
  • ‚ùå Don't soak in water - Extended soaking causes swelling and cracking
  • ‚ùå Don't use abrasive scrubbers - Steel wool or harsh sponges scratch the surface
  • ‚ùå Don't leave in direct sunlight - UV rays dry out and fade the bamboo
  • ‚ùå Don't use in microwave - Bamboo should never be microwaved
  • ‚ùå Don't use bleach or harsh chemicals - These strip natural oils and weaken fibers

‚úÖ Pro Care Tips

  • ‚Ä¢ Remove stains: Use lemon juice + salt paste, scrub gently, rinse well
  • ‚Ä¢ Eliminate odors: Soak in vinegar-water solution (1:4 ratio) for 5 minutes
  • ‚Ä¢ Restore shine: Buff with food-grade mineral oil after cleaning
  • ‚Ä¢ Prevent warping: Always dry immediately after washing

‚è±Ô∏è Expected Lifespan

With proper daily care and monthly conditioning, your Wooden Spoon Set can last 5-7 years or more. Signs it's time to replace: deep cracks, splinters, persistent stains, or loss of structural integrity.
